CHAPTER 10

The Stone Door

Brulgwyn steps forward, gripping the Root-shaped carving in his calloused hand. The torchlight flickers across the ancient stone door, casting long shadows over the worn dwarven script.

With a steady breath, he places the Root into the small, hollow depression at the center of the door.

Click.

The sound echoes like a hammer striking an anvil.

The runes on the door flare to life—soft amber light pulses outward, and the entire stone surface begins to shift with ancient magic. Dust and time peel away as the doorslowly rumbles open, revealing a narrow stairwell descending deeper into the mountain.

From within comes a sudden rush of cold air, carrying with it the scent of earth, decay… and something old and powerful.

But there is no turning back now.

A solid roll, Brulgwyn. (15 + 3 = 18) — that's a very sharp eye and seasoned instinct at work.

As you survey the doorway and descending passage, you pick up on several important details:

  • The first few stepsare slick with moisture—likely condensation from deeper below. You adjust your footing accordingly.
  • On the left wall, faint scratch marks—claw-like—hint that something has climbed out of this passage before.
  • Near the floor ahead, you notice subtle grooves—a pressure plate likely linked to a trap. Without your careful scan, it would’ve triggered.

You carefully bypass it, marking the edge with a bit of chalk from your pack.

Most importantly, you get the impression that you’re being watched—not actively threatened, but observed... evaluated.

Brulgwyn tightens his grip on the haft of his newly acquired warhammer, its polished head gleaming dully in the torchlight. The carved handle fits snugly in his calloused hands—an extension of his will and his wrath.

With quiet, deliberate steps, he begins the descent into the depths of Black Hollow. The air grows colder, and the stone walls close in around him. The only sounds are the distant drip of water and the faint whisper of something ancient shifting below.

As you descend, your torchlight flickers across dwarven murals, cracked and faded: proud halls, blazing forges, and finally—scenes of darkness creeping in. Shadows overtaking light. One final image: a great tree, with blackened roots choking a golden heart.

Eventually, the stairway opens into a wide underground chamber. Pillars line the walls, many cracked or toppled. In the center stands a stone dais—and upon it, something faintly glowing, pulsing like a slow heartbeat…

Brulgwyn has entered the Hollow Heart.

But he’s not alone.

A low growl echoes through the chamber. From the shadows behind a column, two twisted, spectral dwarves emerge—ghostly, with glowing eyes and rusted armor fused to their undead forms. One raises a blackened axe. The other points at you.

““You do not belong here,” they rasp. “Turn back, or be judged like the rest.””

Brulgwyn steps forward with firm resolve, lifting the amulet of Thramdor high above his head. The dwarven runes etched into its surface catch the torchlight, casting faint silver reflections across the chamber.

His voice echoes with both authority and reverence:

““By Thramdor, Hammer-Father and Guardian of the Deep Halls, I walk these stones not in greed, but in duty! My kin suffer, cursed by rot and shadow—I seek the Root not for power, but for salvation!””

The spectral dwarves freeze.

For a tense moment, silence stretches across the chamber—until the amulet begins to glow with soft golden light. The undead figures recoil slightly, eyes narrowing.

Then, one lowers his axe. His voice shifts, no longer hostile—just… tired.

““The oath still echoes… the vow still burns. If your heart is true, and your hammer strikes for the living... pass, kin-blooded. But know this: the Root does not yield easily. It hungers. It remembers.””

The spirits fade into mist, their duty fulfilled—for now.

The chamber is now still. At the center, the stone dais holds a twisting mass of blackened root, pulsing faintly. This must be it—the Root of Withering. The cursed source.

But as Brulgwyn steps closer… he feels something stir within the Root. A presence.
Watching. Waiting.
