CHAPTER 13

The Chamber

Brulgwyn steadies his breath, torchlight flickering against the ancient stone walls as he steps away from the glowing vial—for now. Though the trial has ended, the chamber still hums with a lingering energy, old and layered like the rings of a deep rooted tree.

You scan the chamber with a careful eye.

🕯 With your past Perception and Investigation successes (19 and 20 respectively), you notice:

  • Engravings along the chamber walls now glow faintly in response to the Root's destruction. They depict a ritual—a circle of dwarves holding aloft crystals similar in shape to the Root, each surrounded by a halo of divine energy. At the center stands a figure robed in light, bearing the amulet of Thramdor, very similar to your own.

  • In a recess near the altar, you uncover a small, locked stone compartment. The lock appears mechanical, not magical.

  • Near the collapsed section of wall to the east, a wind stirs faintly from behind the stones—hinting at another hidden path or chamber.

  • The pillar at the center of the chamber has grooves shaped for placing a weapon—likely ceremonial, suggesting this room once served as a temple, or trial hall, for Thramdor's chosen.

Brulgwyn narrows his eyes at the locked stone compartment near the altar. Its ancient mechanism shows signs of wear, but the lock is still intact. He kneels down, steadying his calloused fingers and reaching into his pouch for a set of basic tools—rudimentary, but serviceable.

With a quiet click, Brulgwyn's steady hands manage to shift the ancient locking mechanism. The stone compartment grinds open just enough to reveal its contents: a small, dust-covered satchel embroidered with faded dwarven runes and a sealed vial filled with swirling silvery-blue liquid. The air smells faintly of lavender and ozone.

Inside the satchel, Brulgwyn finds:

  • A Dwarven Prayer Bead (grants +1 to Wisdom saving throws when worn)

  • The Vial of Aetherbliss (restores 4d4+4 HP and cures one disease or poison when consumed)

There’s also a parchment scroll, brittle with age, detailing a ritual of purification— incomplete, but hinting that it can be completed with the knowledge Brulgwyn already

carries from Lady Vaelora and the amulet of Thramdor.

With a total of 15 on your Investigation check, Brulgwyn closely examines the chamber with a discerning eye.

He notices that the stone walls are covered in faint carvings—most worn down by time, but still legible to a careful observer. These carvings depict dwarves kneeling around a glowing root-like structure, with spectral shapes hovering above them. Toward the far end of the chamber, Brulgwyn finds a cracked floor tile slightly misaligned with the others. Beneath it lies a shallow compartment containing:

  • A fragment of ancient parchment, etched with runes similar to those on the Root of Withering.

  • A small vial of silvery liquid, faintly shimmering in the torchlight—perhaps a potion or ritual component.

It seems this hidden cache was part of a forgotten cleansing rite... but it may need more to complete.

Brulgwyn carefully retrieves the parchment and vial, examining them with reverence and focus.

📜The Ancient Parchment

The parchment is brittle but intact, inscribed with flowing dwarvish runes and sacred symbols. As Brulgwyn deciphers what he can, he realizes it is part of a cleansing incantation—a ritual meant to bind and suppress dark corruption from sacred roots.

Key phrases include:

  • “By oath and flame, purge the shadow’s stain.”

  • “Let the heart of stone bear the burden of light.”

  • “When root and spirit align, redemption shall bloom.”

The ritual seems to require:

  • The Root of Withering

  • A cleansing agent (possibly the vial)

  • A divine vow or "oathbound purpose"

🧪The Vial of Silvery Liquid

As Brulgwyn unseals the vial and sniffs it cautiously, he detects a faint mix of sacred herbs, blessed oil, and possibly moon-touched silver. This is no ordinary potion— it appears to be a distilled purifying essence, likely meant to counteract spiritual or necrotic corruption.

When combined with the Root and the incantation, this could form a ritual of sanctification—but only if performed with conviction and the proper words.

Very wise—Brulgwyn takes a moment to assess the vial’s contents more deeply and ensure it's safe and effective for ritual use. Let’s see how the roll plays out.

With a Medicine check of 16, Brulgwyn carefully examines the vial's contents. He uncorks it slightly, letting a faint, silvery mist waft up—tinged with a subtle herbal scent. His experience with both battlefield wounds and herbal remedies tells him this is not a typical potion. It’s alchemically refined and likely safe, but its magic is unstable.

You deduce:

  • It’s a restorative elixir, possibly tied to cleansing or resistance.

  • The instability suggests it should be used during a ritual or in a sacred place, rather than drunk casually.

  • There's a faint resonance with the Root of Withering, almost as if this vial was designed to counterbalance its corruption.

The engraved band around the vial reads:

““By flame and stone, let rot be undone.””

This could be a verbal component for activating it.

Brulgwyn kneels before the altar, torchlight flickering across the ancient stone as he sets the vial gently at its center. He places the Root of Withering beside it—still dark and pulsing faintly with cursed energy. The engraved words echo in his memory:

““By flame and stone, let rot be undone.””

He grips the amulet of Thramdor and begins the ritual.

[Ritual Scene — Skill Check Required]

To perform the ritual, Brulgwyn must draw on three forces:

  1. His connection to divine protection (Religion)

  2. His understanding of the corrupted artifact (Arcana)

  3. His physical steadiness to complete the rite without mistake (Constitution Saving Throw)

Brulgwyn's Ritual Attempt

The scene is intense, and the ritual requires both mental and physical endurance. Here’s how the rolls affect the outcome:

  • Religion (2 + 0 = 2) Brulgwyn’s call to Thramdor falters. His heart feels heavy, and the divine energy he's trying to invoke slips through his grasp, like water through his fingers. The connection to the gods feels distant, and the weight of his mission brings doubt into his heart. The amulet of Thramdor pulses weakly, but the divine light is not as strong as it should be.

  • Arcana (15 + 0 = 15) Brulgwyn gains insight into the magical nature of the Root of Withering. He realizes that while he’s successful in attempting to harness the energies of the vial, the potion itself is far more delicate than he anticipated. It's unstable and volatile, so any incorrect use could make things worse. However, he understands enough to proceed cautiously with the rite.

  • Wisdom Saving Throw (3 + 1 = 4) As Brulgwyn attempts to endure the strain of the ritual, a wave of psychic pressure overwhelms him. His mind aches as he struggles to concentrate on both the ritual and the heavy burden of saving his village. The world seems to shrink, and his resolve falters under the immense weight of the curse. The pressure of the ritual causes him to stumble and lose focus.
    •

    Consequences:

    • The ritual is partially successful, but not without consequence.

    • The vial’s energy reacts to the Root of Withering, but the lack of divine strength causes the curse to shift into a more unstable state.

    • Brulgwyn feels a wave of exhaustion and mental fog, but he has bought himself some time in the process. The room grows colder, the air heavier, as the lingering curse tries to reclaim what was undone.
