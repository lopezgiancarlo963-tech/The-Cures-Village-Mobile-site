CHAPTER 5

Black Hollow

Brulgwyn furrows his brow, staring at the crude drawing of the Root of Withering before looking back at Eldrin. "Tell me about Black Hollow. What am I walking into?"

Eldrin exhales slowly, placing a hand on the dusty tome. “Black Hollow was once a prosperous dwarven settlement, built deep within the ashen cliffs far to the north. Its halls were carved into the mountain itself, rich with ore and dwarven craft.”

He pauses, his expression darkening. “But something happened. The records are unclear, but the settlement was abandoned centuries ago, its name erased from most dwarven histories. The only stories that remain speak of shadows creeping through the halls, voices whispering in the dark… and a great calamity that befell its people.”

He taps a finger on the Root of Withering’s illustration. “If this artifact is there, someone must have unearthed it. And whoever did… may have unleashed the curse upon your village.”

Eldrin folds his arms. “Black Hollow is not a place for the weak-hearted. You will face more than just old stone and dust. The past is restless there.”

Brulgwyn crosses his arms, his expression unwavering. “If the past is restless, I need to know what I’m walking into. What dangers lurk in Black Hollow?” Eldrin’s gaze sharpens, and he steps closer to the flickering brazier. The green embers cast eerie shadows across his face as he speaks.

“The first danger is the land itself.” He waves a hand, and the fire crackles, shifting into the shape of jagged cliffs and a ruined city. "Black Hollow lies deep in the northern mountains, where the winds howl like the dead. Few roads lead there, and those that do are treacherous. Avalanches, sheer drops, and frozen tunnels will be your first test."

The flames flicker again, reshaping into twisted, shadowy figures.

“But worse still are the spirits.”

Eldrin’s voice lowers. “Something unnatural happened in Black Hollow. The dwarves who lived there did not simply vanish—they linger. Shades of the past, trapped between worlds, bound by sorrow and rage. If they sense your presence, they may not let you leave.”

The fire shifts once more, revealing a massive, slithering shape in the depths of the ruins.

“And then… there are the deeper things.” Eldrin’s voice grows grim. “The calamity that befell Black Hollow was not the work of mere men. Some say the settlement uncovered something beneath the stone—a darkness that should have remained buried. If the Root of Withering is tied to this, then whatever lies beneath may still be waiting.”

He lets the fire die down, turning back to Brulgwyn. “So I ask you, warrior—do you still intend to go?”

Brulgwyn crosses his arms, his brow furrowed in thought. “If this curse is ancient, surely there must be another way to break it. Could a powerful healer, cleric, or wizard undo its effects?”

Eldrin strokes his beard, considering the question. “A powerful enough divine healer or arcane master might be able to cleanse the curse… but such magic is rare, and the deeper the corruption, the harder it is to undo. If the Root of Withering is truly the source, then simply healing the sick might not be enough—it must be destroyed, or the curse could return.”

He taps his fingers on the wooden table. “However… there may be one who could help.”

He turns to another shelf, pulling free a faded map and tracing a path with his finger. “Deep within the Emerald Vale, past the silver rivers, dwells an ancient healer—Lady Vaelora, the Last Light. She is said to wield magic strong enough to cleanse even the blackest corruption.”

Eldrin looks back at Brulgwyn. “The journey is perilous. The Vale is shrouded in enchantments, and many who seek it never return. But if you wish to find another path, seeking Lady Vaelora may be your best hope.”
