Failed Ritual

Brulgwyn had used the sacred vial and attempted a ritual to purify the Root of Withering. The effort strained his mind and spirit, leaving him weakened—but not defeated. The curse was not fully cleansed, but its corruption had been delayed. Now, deep in the Hollow Heart of the ruins, flickers of pale green light pulse faintly from the partially purified root.

Before him, the chamber is quiet—too quiet. The ancient dwarven symbols carved into the walls glow faintly, and the air hums with buried power. There is a narrow corridor to the right, newly revealed when the ritual pulse passed through the room like a heartbeat.

  • Carrying the amulet of Thramdor and warhammer

With an Arcana check of 15, Brulgwyn focuses on the residual energies pulsing faintly in the chamber. The air carries a subtle hum—an echo of magic still active, but changed. The purification ritual, while incomplete, has weakened the Root of Withering’s hold, muting the corruption that once saturated the walls.

You sense that:

  • The vial you used resonated with ancient elven enchantments, likely tied to Lady Vaelora’s lineage.

  • The magic is unstable, and if left too long, the Root may reawaken.

  • A final sealing ritual or divine catalyst—perhaps involving a relic or direct blessing from Thramdor—could end the curse for good.

Faint etchings on the stone floor now glow dimly. They form a sigil of Thramdor, pointing to a deeper sanctum beyond the cracked door to the south.

Brulgwyn grips his warhammer and steps carefully toward the sigil, torchlight flickering off ancient stone. The glowing rune pulses faintly with each step he takes, as if recognizing the presence of the amulet of Thramdor at his chest. As he crosses the threshold, the cracked stone door creaks open—unbidden—and reveals a spiraling descent into the Sanctum of the Hollow Heart.

The air grows colder. Carvings along the walls depict the fall of Black Hollow— dwarves succumbing to shadow, roots tangling through stone, and a final desperate plea to their god. At the base of the spiral, Brulgwyn finds a massive chamber, shaped like a heart split by a jagged fissure. At its center rests a twisted root throne, where a shriveled dwarven figure slumps—eyes glowing with a faint, unnatural light.

The figure slowly raises its head and speaks in a rasping voice:

“"You carry Thramdor’s light... yet this place is bound to rot and sorrow. Speak, dwarf—do you come to banish me... or to understand me?"”

The air grows thick with tension, as the spirit’s essence claws at the edges of your mind.

With an Insight check of 8, Brulgwyn narrows his eyes at the spectral figure, trying to read its emotions or intent—but the being’s expression remains inscrutable. The sorrow in its voice could be genuine, or it could be an illusion, a manipulation crafted by the lingering magic of the Hollow.

The figure gestures faintly toward the sigils along the path ahead and says:

"The path you follow is lined with echoes of what once was. But not all truths are buried—some still breathe in shadows."

The sigils ahead faintly glow, pulsing like a heartbeat in the darkness.

With a Perception check of 10, Brulgwyn cautiously surveys the corridor ahead. The glowing sigils etched into the stone walls continue to pulse with a faint amber light, guiding him deeper into the Hollow Heart. Though the path remains dim, he notices:

  • Faint boot prints in the dust—old and barely visible—suggesting someone walked this way long ago.

  • The sigils seem to react subtly to his presence, flickering ever so slightly when he steps near them.

  • A chill lingers in the air, and the sound of distant whispers rises and falls, as if the very stones hold memories of the past.

The way ahead curves into a larger chamber, and the pulsing light seems to intensify slightly as he approaches. Something lies beyond—something old, sacred, and bound to the curse.

Brulgwyn tightens his grip on his warhammer and steps into the larger chamber beyond the sigil-marked corridor.

The room opens wide, its high, arched ceiling held aloft by blackened stone pillars carved with dwarven runes long faded by time and decay. At the center stands a massive stone basin, glowing faintly with the same amber hue as the sigils. Suspended above it, held in midair by unseen magic, is a twisted root, pulsing darkly —its tendrils coiled and writhing like veins of shadow. This is undoubtedly the Heart of the Root of Withering.

Surrounding the basin are four statues of dwarven clerics, each holding a relic: a hammer, a scroll, a chalice, and a flame. The air is heavy with ancient power, and the whispers Brulgwyn heard earlier now form fragmented chants, like prayers fractured by time.

Suddenly, a deep, echoing voice fills the chamber—not from a figure, but from the root itself:

"Bearer of Thramdor... why do you trespass in the Hollow Heart?"

The root pulses, awaiting Brulgwyn’s answer.

Brulgwyn says " I came here to find a cure for my people of my town, I came here with no harm but with peace"

The chamber seems to hold its breath.

The pulsing root quivers, and the voice returns—quieter this time, as if sifting through Brulgwyn’s soul:

“Peace... is rare among those who seek power. But you carry not ambition— only burden.”

The statues around the basin glow faintly. The cleric with the chalice emits a soft, silvery light that flows toward the basin. The tendrils around the root recoil slightly, and the chants shift to a more harmonious tone—still eerie, but no longer menacing.

The voice speaks again:

“To cure what has been tainted, you must complete the Rite of Four Pillars. Each relic must be awakened with truth. Speak the tenets you uphold, and we shall see if you are worthy.”

The relics—the hammer, scroll, chalice, and flame—now glow dimly, awaiting Brulgwyn’s approach.

Which relic will Brulgwyn approach first, and what truth will he offer it?

The voice returns, now resonating from the stone:

“This is the Pillar of Strength. Not of muscle, but of purpose. What truth do you forge in your heart that grants you the strength to endure?”

The hammer glows faintly, awaiting Brulgwyn’s answer. A truthful declaration— personal, unwavering—will awaken it.
